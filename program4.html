WEIGHT LIFTING in Gym

Summary

Weight lifting, also known as strength training or resistance training, involves exercises that challenge your muscles by using resistance. This resistance can come from free weights, weight machines, or your own body weight12.

Advantages

Increases muscle mass: Builds lean muscle, which can boost metabolism3. Strengthens bones: Helps improve bone density, reducing the risk of osteoporosis3. Improves endurance: Increases muscular endurance, making daily activities easier3. Enhances mental health: Can boost self-esteem and reduce symptoms of depression and anxiety4.

Disadvantages

Risk of injury: Incorrect technique can lead to muscle strains or joint issues5. Muscle imbalance: Overtraining certain muscle groups while neglecting others can cause imbalances5. Plateaus: Without progressively increasing resistance, you may stop seeing improvements6.

Aims

Improving overall strength: To become stronger and more capable in physical tasks3. Enhancing physical appearance: Many people lift weights to improve their body composition and aesthetics3. Boosting sports performance: Athletes often use weight lifting to improve their performance in sports3.

How to Do

Start with a warm-up: To prepare your muscles and reduce the risk of injury7. Learn proper form: This is crucial to prevent injuries and ensure effectiveness. Consider consulting a personal trainer when starting out7. Choose the right weight: Start with lighter weights and increase gradually as you become stronger7. Follow a routine: Focus on different muscle groups and include a variety of exercises7. Cool down: Stretch after your workout to aid recovery and flexibility7. Always remember to listen to your body and consult with healthcare professionals before starting any new exercise regimen, especially if you have pre-existing health conditions
